Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common logistics and shipping questions.

Volumetric (or dimensional) weight is calculated by multiplying the length, width, and height of a package in centimeters and dividing by a standard volumetric factor (usually 5000 for air cargo). The shipping rate is based on the higher value between the volumetric weight and the actual physical weight.
Standard customs documents include the Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading (for ocean) or Air Waybill (for air), and a Certificate of Origin. Specialized cargo (e.g. food, chemicals, medicine) may require extra regulatory approvals or import licenses.
FCL (Full Container Load) means you book an entire container (20ft or 40ft) exclusively for your cargo. LCL (Less than Container Load) consolidates your cargo with other shippers' goods in the same container, paying only for the volume (cubic meters) your shipment occupies.
Yes, we offer standard carrier liability. However, to guarantee full coverage against accidental loss or damage during transit, we strongly recommend purchasing comprehensive third-party cargo insurance, which we can organize for your shipment during booking.
